Frequently Asked Questions

What's included in every package?
Every package includes mobile-responsive design, basic SEO setup (title tags, meta descriptions, sitemap), support for your custom domain, and up to 2 rounds of revisions. You own everything — no recurring fees unless you add maintenance.
Do you offer payment plans?
Yes. For projects over $399, we typically split it 50% upfront and 50% on delivery. If you need a different arrangement, just ask — we're flexible with small businesses.
How long does a site take to build?
Starter sites usually take 5–7 days. Business sites run 10–12 days. Pro and E-Commerce projects are typically 2–3 weeks. The biggest factor is how quickly you can provide content (photos, info, etc.).
What if I need changes after launch?
Minor changes in the first 7 days after launch are always free. After that, small fixes are usually quick and we quote them honestly. Or sign up for Monthly Maintenance for ongoing peace of mind.
Do I need to buy hosting or a domain?
You'll need a domain name (usually ~$12/yr from Namecheap or Google Domains). For hosting, we can set you up on a free tier like Netlify or GitHub Pages for most sites, or advise on affordable paid options if needed.

Why Every Small Business Needs a Website in 2026

Your customers are Googling you before they walk in the door — or they're walking past. Here's why having no website is the most expensive mistake a small business can make right now.

In 2026, 78% of consumers look up a business online before visiting in person. If they can't find you — or worse, they find a site that looks like it hasn't been updated since 2009 — they move on to whoever's next in the search results.

A website isn't a luxury anymore. It's your storefront, your business card, and your first impression all rolled into one. And unlike a physical sign, it works 24/7 without you having to do anything.

Social media isn't enough. Facebook and Instagram can change their algorithm any day. Your business profile gets buried. But your website? That's yours. No algorithm decides whether people see it. When someone types your business name into Google, your site shows up.

Even a simple, clean, one-page site with your services, contact info, and a few photos does more for credibility than any number of social posts. It tells customers you're real, you're professional, and you mean business.

The businesses that skipped getting a website in 2020 lost ground during the pandemic and never fully got it back. Don't make the same call in 2026. A site that costs a few hundred dollars today can easily be worth thousands in new customers over the next year.

5 Signs It's Time to Redesign Your Website

If your site takes forever to load, looks bad on phones, or you're embarrassed to hand out your URL — it's time. Here's how to know for sure and what to do about it.

1. You're embarrassed to share it. If you hesitate before giving out your website URL, that hesitation is costing you business. Your site should be something you're proud to hand to a new customer.

2. It looks bad on mobile. Over 60% of web traffic now comes from phones. If your site isn't responsive — meaning it resizes and reflows for small screens — you're losing more than half your potential visitors the moment they land on it.

3. It loads slowly. Every extra second of load time drops your conversion rate. If your site uses outdated technology, heavy unoptimized images, or old page builders, it's probably slow — and Google penalizes that in search rankings.

4. It doesn't reflect who you are anymore. Businesses evolve. If your site still shows your old logo, old services, or old prices, it's not just outdated — it's actively misleading customers. A redesign is also a chance to reset your brand.

5. You can't update it yourself. If your site is a black box that only a specific developer can touch, that's a problem. Modern sites should be easy to update, or at minimum, have a clear process for making changes without a full rebuild.

Any one of these is a good reason to act. If two or more apply to you, the redesign will pay for itself faster than you think.

Template vs. Custom: What's Actually the Difference?

Templates are cheap and fast. Custom sites are built for you. But what does that actually mean for your business? Here's the honest breakdown — no sales pitch.

When people say "template website," they usually mean something built on Squarespace, Wix, or a WordPress theme. You pick a layout, swap in your photos and text, and you're live. It's genuinely fast and costs close to nothing upfront.

The trade-off: you're renting space in someone else's box. Every business using that template looks vaguely similar. You can't move things around freely. The page builder adds layers of code that slow things down. And every month, you pay a subscription just to keep the site alive.

Custom-coded sites start from scratch in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Every element is placed intentionally. The code is clean and loads fast. Nothing is there by default — only what your site actually needs.

That means the site can look exactly like your brand, not like a theme that thousands of other businesses are also using. It also means you own the files outright — no platform can raise prices on you or shut down and take your site with them.

For a solo freelancer or brand-new startup, a template might be the right call to get online quickly with zero budget. But for a business that's serious about its brand and wants to stay competitive long-term, custom is almost always the smarter investment — especially when the price difference isn't as big as most people assume.
